‚óŹ Here is the mechanism, end-to-end.

  The fractionation pipeline

  Each timestep, a gabbroic-magma marker has three possible fates in this order:
  1. Extraction (MagmaBody.jl:197) ‚ÄĒ sets matid = ExtractedGabbroicMagma.
  2. Fractionation (Fractionation.jl:110-125) ‚ÄĒ if matid == 
  ExtractedGabbroicMagma AND |y_marker ‚ąí y_moho| < fractionation_threshold_limit
   (default 2000 m, e.g. spreading_center/Model.jl:348), then matid = 
  ExtractedLayeredGabbroicMagma.
  3. Solidification (Solidification.jl:76-86) ‚ÄĒ once meltfrac < 1:
    - ExtractedGabbroicMagma ‚Üí SolidifiedGabbro (or its partially-molten
  variant)
    - ExtractedLayeredGabbroicMagma ‚Üí SolidifiedLayeredGabbro (or its
  partially-molten variant)

  The fractionation loop's gate is if matid == matid_gabbroic_magma ‚ÄĒ once a 
  marker solidifies, it can never become layered. That's the trapdoor.

  Why early-time Moho is artificially shallow

  calculate_oceanic_moho (Fractionation.jl:139-159) computes the Moho as the
  bottom of all oceanic-crust matids. Two things conspire at early times:

  1. ExtractedGabbroicMagma is NOT in get_matids_oceanic_crust
  (Fractionation.jl:206-220). So while the chamber is mostly still molten
  un-fractionated gabbroic magma, the magma chamber itself doesn't anchor the
  Moho. The Moho is instead pinned to the bottom of the small amount of
  solidified gabbro / basalt / sediment / extruded magma / layered material that
   already exists.
  2. Partial-melt-domain clamp (Fractionation.jl:106): moho_gridy .= 
  min.(moho_gridy, partial_melt_gridy). With y increasing downward, this forces
  the Moho to be no deeper than the top of the mantle partial-melt domain. Early
   in the simulation the PMZ sits very shallow (and is computed from
  UltramaficMantlePartiallyMolten, Drainage.jl:114), so the Moho is yanked up.

  Net result at step 22: the Moho is sitting near the surface of the chamber,
  not near its base.

  Consequence at step 22

  - New gabbroic-magma markers are getting injected into the base of the chamber
   (MagmaBody.jl:197 works on the shallowest partially-molten mantle marker,
  which migrates down as the chamber fills).
  - At the chamber base, |y_marker ‚ąí y_moho| is much larger than 2 km, so they
  are not fractionated.
  - They cool, meltfrac drops below 1, and they pass through the solidification
  gate as SolidifiedGabbro / SolidifiedGabbroPartiallyMolten. That trapdoor is
  now closed ‚ÄĒ they cannot retroactively become layered.

  That partially-molten gabbro band at the base in step 22 is exactly that
  cohort: gabbroic magma that solidified before the Moho ever got close enough
  to it.

  Why step 24 looks correct

  By 4.6 Myr the column is well-developed: solidified basalt, extruded gabbroic
  magma, and solidified layered gabbro now provide good Moho samples in
  bottom_oc, AND the partial-melt-top has moved deeper (or moved laterally) so
  the min clamp no longer pulls the Moho artificially shallow. The Moho lands
  close to the actual chamber base, the 2 km window catches new gabbroic-magma
  markers before they solidify, and fractionation works as designed.

  The old "trapped" gabbro from step 22 is still there ‚ÄĒ it just isn't visible
  inside the same zoom-in by step 24 because it has been spread laterally by
  half-spreading and is sitting under the older crust off to the sides (or has
  descended below the cropped frame).

  Summary

  Early in the run the Moho used by fractionation is too shallow ‚ÄĒ both because
  the bulk of the new crust is ExtractedGabbroicMagma (excluded from the Moho
  calculation) and because the shallow mantle PMZ clamps the Moho upward.
  Gabbroic-magma markers at the chamber base are outside the 2 km fractionation
  window, so they solidify as plain gabbro before they ever get a chance to be
  relabelled layered. Once solidified, the marker is permanently outside the
  fractionation gate.

‚óŹ Good catch ‚ÄĒ yes, the algorithm DOES include SolidifiedGabbroPartiallyMolten
  in matids_oc (Fractionation.jl:211), so in principle the Moho should land at
  the base of that 2‚Äď2.5 km partially-molten-gabbro zone, putting the thin
  molten-gabbro lens well within the 2 km threshold. The reason it doesn't is
  the post-processing of the Moho surface, not the matid list. Two effects
  compound:

  1. Empty columns get filled with the topography depth

  After calculate_top_and_bottom_of_layer_opt (Fractionation.jl:153-156), any
  column where no oceanic-crust marker was found returns bottom_oc = 0.0. Then
  set_zero_values_to_topoy (Fractionation.jl:228-237) overwrites those zeros
  with the topography depth ‚ÄĒ i.e., the very shallow surface elevation.

  So at step 22, in every x-column outside the narrow oceanic-crust footprint
  above the chamber, bottom_oc is being set to ~surface depth (a few hundred
  meters), not to a deep value. Inside the footprint, bottom_oc ‚Čą base of the
  partially-molten-gabbro zone (deep ‚ÄĒ multiple km).

  This produces a sharp step in bottom_oc at the edges of the oceanic-crust
  footprint.

  2. A 10 km smoothing window flattens that step

  calculate_oceanic_moho then calls smooth_surface(bottom_oc, nsmooth=nsmooth)
  (Fractionation.jl:158) where:

  nsmooth = floor(smoothing_radius_fractionation / dx_grid)
          = floor(10_000 / dx_grid)              #
  smoothing_radius_fractionation default = 10 km

  smooth_surface (MathTools.jl:250-266) is a uniform box average over ¬Īnsmooth 
  nodes, i.e. a window roughly 20 km wide. At 4.2 Myr the
  partially-molten-gabbro footprint is only a few km wide, so every column in
  the centre of the footprint averages a small number of "deep" values together
  with a large number of "topography-depth" values from the surrounding empty
  columns.

  Result: the smoothed Moho in the centre of the chamber is dragged tens of
  percent of the way up toward the topography ‚ÄĒ well above the actual base of
  the partially-molten-gabbro zone. In a typical configuration, that yanks it up
   by 2‚Äď4 km, easily enough to push the molten-gabbro lens at the true base
  outside the 2 km fractionation_threshold_limit.

  3. The partial-melt-domain clamp doesn't save it

  moho_gridy .= min.(moho_gridy, partial_melt_gridy) (Fractionation.jl:106) only
   pushes the Moho shallower, never deeper. The top of the mantle partial-melt
  domain is below the lens, so the clamp is inactive (the smoothed moho_gridy is
   already shallower than partial_melt_gridy).

  Net effect

  The fractionation Moho is not the geometric base of the oceanic-crust column ‚ÄĒ
   it is a heavily-smoothed version that loses fidelity wherever the
  oceanic-crust footprint is narrow compared to smoothing_radius_fractionation
  (10 km by default). At step 22 the footprint is narrow, so the Moho is pulled
  up into the partially-molten-gabbro zone itself; the lens sits more than 2 km
  below the smoothed Moho and fails the threshold check at every step until it
  solidifies. By step 24 the footprint has grown wide enough relative to the 10
  km kernel that the smoothed Moho lands close to the true base, and new
  gabbroic-magma markers fractionate as expected.
